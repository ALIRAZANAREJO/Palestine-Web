Squad / Section

The Squad or Section in Israel refers to a small military unit within the Israel Defense Forces (IDF), typically consisting of around 8 to 12 soldiers, led by a squad leader.

Platoonn

A Platoon in Israel refers to a mid-sized military unit within the Israel Defense Forces (IDF), typically consisting of around 20 to 30 soldiers, led by a lieutenant or second lieutenant.

Company

A Company in Israel refers to a larger military unit within the Israel Defense Forces (IDF), typically consisting of around 100 to 200 soldiers, commanded by a captain or major.

Artillery Battery

An Artillery Battery in Israel refers to a specialized military unit within the Israel Defense Forces (IDF), responsible for operating and deploying artillery pieces, such as howitzers or rocket launchers, to provide indirect fire support during combat operations.

Reconnaissance

Reconnaissance in Israel refers to the specialized military activities conducted by units within the Israel Defense Forces (IDF) to gather intelligence, assess enemy movements, and provide critical information for operational planning and decision-making.

Battalion

A Battalion in Israel refers to a large military unit within the Israel Defense Forces (IDF), typically consisting of around 300 to 800 soldiers, commanded by a lieutenant colonel or a colonel, and composed of several companies.

Brigade

A Brigade in Israel refers to a sizable military unit within the Israel Defense Forces (IDF), comprising several battalions and specialized units, and commanded by a brigadier general or a colonel.

Division

A Division in Israel refers to a significant military formation within the Israel Defense Forces (IDF), encompassing multiple brigades and support units, and typically commanded by a major general.

Army

The Army of Israel refers to the entire military force of the country, including ground, air, and naval components, responsible for national defense and security.

Fire Team

The Fire Team in Israel refers to a small tactical unit within the Israel Defense Forces (IDF), typically consisting of around four to six members. These teams play a crucial role in infantry operations, including maneuvering, reconnaissance, and engaging enemy targets.
