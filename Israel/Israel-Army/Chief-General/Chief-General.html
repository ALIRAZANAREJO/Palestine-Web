Cheif General Aluf Herzi Halevi OF israel

Chiefs of Staff

On January 16, 2023, Halevi assumed the prestigious position of Chief of the General Staff of the IDF, the highest rank in the Israeli military hierarchy At any given time, the Chief of the General Staff is the only active officer holding the IDF's highest rank, rav aluf (רַב־אַלּוּף‎), which is usually translated into English as lieutenant general, a three-star rank. The only exception to this rule occurred during the Yom Kippur War, when former Chief of the General Staff Haim Bar-Lev, who was a cabinet member at the outbreak of and during the war, was brought out of retirement and installed as chief of Southern Command.[1] For a brief period, he and Chief of the General Staff David Elazar were both in active service with the rank of rav aluf.

Deputy Chiefs of Staff

Prior to his current role, Halevi served as the commander of the Israeli Southern Command. This command oversees the southern borders of Israel, a region marked by its unique security dynamics. From the Sinai Peninsula to the Gaza Strip, Halevi navigated complex geopolitical landscapes. His tenure witnessed heightened tensions, border skirmishes, and the delicate balance between security and humanitarian concerns.

Heads of the Israeli Military
Intelligence Directorate (Aman)

Halevi’s expertise extended to intelligence operations. He held the crucial position of chief of the Military Intelligence Directorate (Aman). In this role, he was responsible for gathering critical information, analyzing threats, and safeguarding national security. His insights influenced strategic decisions at the highest levels. The Aman’s work involves monitoring neighboring countries, assessing military capabilities, and providing timely intelligence to policymakers.

Heads of Southern Command

Halevi’s journey began as a paratrooper. He commanded the 35th Paratroopers Brigade, a storied unit with a legacy of valor and resilience. His leadership skills were further honed during his time with the elite Sayeret Matkal, Israel’s premier special forces unit. Sayeret Matkal operates covertly, executing high-risk missions. Halevi’s experiences in these units shaped his understanding of combat, teamwork, and the sacrifices demanded by military service.
